Project Alpha

The Megastructure

Spatial Composition Study

Summary

This level was created initially as a study of spatial composition and image composition that I decided to push beyond initial constraints with new ideas and iterations based on feedback I received from classmates.

Taking inspiration from a manga series I enjoy (Blame!), I tried to create a vast, maze-like, dark, decrepit structure that encouraged exploration with rewards and unlockable areas.

Role

Level Design / Scripting

Engine

Unreal Engine 4

Duration

3 Weeks (~80 Hours)

Level Layout

2D Map & Pacing

Map Overview

I wanted to create a space that the Player would constantly revisit as they explored. This helped show the Player their progress through the level regardless of their point of entry, and allowed for reusing the same space in different ways.

Beat Graph

Visual planning of tense moments and pacing throughout the level.

Gameplay Highlights

Active Traversal

Obstacles ensure players are actively interacting with the environment while navigating.

Platforming Challenges

Timed platforms with visual cues add complexity to movement.

Environmental Storytelling

Using lighting and props to guide the player without UI markers.

Stealth Encounters

Designing spaces with alcoves and verticality for player agency.

Reference & Inspiration

Post Mortem

+ What went well?

  • ▹Created a relatively polished level blockout in 3 weeks (80 hours).
  • ▹Incorporated robust interactivity: vents, loot, keys, ladders, and buttons.
  • ▹Iterated significantly based on 3 rounds of peer feedback.

- What could be improved?

  • ▹Some areas felt lackluster; focused too much on visuals over gameplay density.
  • ▹Level flow became more linear than the intended "hub-and-spoke" design.
  • ▹Camera collision issues inside tight vent spaces need technical fixing.
